Initial Research

Research

We did lots of research on the walking mechanics of harvestmen, as well as some of their behaviors, such as eating, forming clumps, and spraying defensive chemicals.

Brainstorming

Brainstorming

We brainstormed different ideas for games/play experiences we could make based on the different behaviors of harvestmen, and found that we came up with the most ideas for the clumping behavior.

Harvestmen Prototypes

prototypes

Some early prototypes of harvestmen for our clumping game, made out of sparkly pom-poms and bent paper clips (only partially kid-friendly).

Wall Prototype

prototypes

A prototype of the wall we would later build as a place for the harvestmen to stick to. The wall is made of pom-poms, and works pretty well with the paper-clip prototypes! Unfortunately, not very cave-like...

Babie Boye

babie boye

Our favorite prototype. We love him so, so much. He's a yarn pom-pom with longer legs of yarn, and bits of velcro stuck to the ends of his legs. He sticks really well to felt, yarn, and other soft materials. He is the cornerstone of our hearts.

Harvestmen Galore!

final project

Harvestmen for our final project! Like Babey Boye, they're yarn and have velcro on their legs. There are 36 of them total, and a matching team of 36 in green. This way, two teams of two students each can compete with each other in order to get as many harvestmen on the wall as possible!
